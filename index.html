Who We Are

Bringing Hope & Education
to Kinamba Since 2008

2008
Year Founded

The Kinamba Community Project provides educational opportunities for the people of the Kamutwa communities in the Kinamba area of Kigali, Rwanda. The devastating genocide of 1994 left war widows, orphans and vulnerable people in desperate need — we exist to offer them hope.

In 2008, we purchased a house and additional buildings in Kinamba, gradually converting them into classrooms, a kitchen, play areas, stores and toilets. Today we serve children from nursery all the way through to university support.

  • Nursery education for children aged 3–6 in different communities
  • Primary school support, homework clubs and feeding programme
  • Secondary school sponsorship and boarding support
  • University pathways for the most academically talented
  • Adult literacy, numeracy and vocational skills training
  • Women's cooperatives — basket weaving, tailoring, jewellery
M
Meg Fletcher
Founding Trustee & CEO
Retired Primary Headteacher from Cumbria, UK. First visited Rwanda in 2006 as a VSO volunteer. Awarded a Points of Light Award for her decade of dedication to Rwanda's children.

Our Journey

From 2006 to Today

Every milestone is a life changed. Here is the story of Kinamba Community Project.

2006
Meg Arrives in Rwanda
Meg Fletcher arrives in Kigali as a VSO volunteer, meeting genocide orphans and war widows in the Kinamba community. Inspired to create lasting change.
2008
Project Founded — Buildings Purchased
UK Charity registered (No. 1126601). A house and smaller buildings purchased in Kinamba, converting them into classrooms, a kitchen, play areas and toilets.
2009
Nursery School Opens
First nursery classes open in Kinamba, welcoming the youngest children of the Kamutwa community. The feeding programme begins to provide daily meals.
2012
Primary Support Programme Expands
Individual child sponsorships grow, supporting over 100 children through primary education in local schools with homework clubs and materials.
2015
Secondary & Adult Programme Launch
Secondary school sponsorships introduced. Adult literacy, numeracy and women's vocational cooperatives established.
2018
330+ Students Enrolled
80 in nursery, 135 in primary support, 53 at secondary level. Meg awarded the UK Points of Light Award.
2022
University Students — A Landmark Moment
Young adults who first joined KCP as small children begin entering university.
2023
10 University Students in a Single Year
A milestone — ten young people progress to university in one year.
2025
Continuing the Mission
KCP continues serving the Kinamba community, inspiring younger children and challenging gender inequality through education.
